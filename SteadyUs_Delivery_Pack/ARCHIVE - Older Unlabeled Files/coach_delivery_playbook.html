Coach Delivery Playbook

This operating guide turns the Couples Budget Reset into a structured delivery asset you can review before calls, use in training, or hand to another coach supporting the same offer.

Working summary

Session 1 helps the couple see the real picture together. Session 2 helps them agree what to do next and how to make that plan work in real life.

Purpose of the offer

The Couples Budget Reset is designed for couples who feel capable but financially disorganised, stretched, or stuck. The work should create calmer conversations, clearer priorities, and a shared plan that feels realistic enough to follow.

Core principleWhat it means in delivery
Calm over pressureThe sessions should feel steady, non-judgemental, and practical rather than intense or corrective.
Clarity over complexityThe goal is not technical analysis. The goal is a clearer shared picture and better decisions.
Shared ownershipBoth partners should leave knowing what happens next rather than one person carrying the mental load alone.
Practical movementEvery session should end with decisions, priorities, or next steps that can actually be used.

Scope boundary

This work covers budgeting support and financial organisation only. It does not include regulated financial, investment, pension, tax, mortgage-product, or legal advice. It is also not debt counselling, therapy, crisis support, or dispute mediation.

If this comes upCoach response
Specialist advice requestAcknowledge it clearly, note that it sits outside scope, and return to the next practical organisational decision inside the coaching remit.
Emotionally loaded conflictReduce blame, slow the conversation, and keep attention on clarity and shared process rather than taking sides.

Overall delivery rhythm

The package works best when the first session creates visibility and the second session creates movement. The first session should not rush into fixing everything. The second session should not reopen every possible issue.

Before Session 1

The goal before the first call is to reduce friction and help the couple arrive ready to talk honestly. Keep the tone personal and low-pressure.

Pre-session aimPractical approach
Lower emotional pressureReassure them that the point is clarity, not judgement.
Set expectationsExplain that Session 1 is about understanding the current picture, not solving everything immediately.
Reduce preparation burdenAsk only for light preparation or simple prompts rather than extensive documents.
Encourage joint attendancePosition the session as shared work rather than one partner being assessed.

Session 1: money clarity

Session 1 is designed to slow the conversation down and make the current situation visible. The core objective is to help both partners see the same picture at the same time.

StageWhat to doWhat good looks like
Open the conversationSet a calm tone, explain the session aim, and reinforce that the goal is clarity rather than blame.Both partners feel they can contribute without being judged.
Map the current money pictureBring income, spending, savings, obligations, and current pressures into one shared view.The couple can describe their situation in simple, concrete terms.
Surface pressure pointsNotice where money feels stretched, avoided, emotionally loaded, or repeatedly postponed.Hidden stress becomes discussable rather than vague.
Identify friction patternsHighlight recurring loops such as avoidance, confusion, overload, uneven responsibility, or repeated disagreements.The couple can name what keeps happening and why it matters.
Close with focusSummarise the few issues that matter most and frame what Session 2 needs to solve next.They leave with clarity rather than overwhelm.

Between sessions and Session 2

The gap between sessions should keep momentum alive without creating homework overload. Session 2 should then turn understanding into usable decisions.

StageCoach focusDesired result
Between sessionsSend simple prompts, keep the work manageable, and help the couple notice what matters most.Session 2 begins with momentum rather than drift.
Reconnect to Session 1Restate the main pressure points and confirm what feels most important now.The second session feels focused and connected.
Agree realistic prioritiesNarrow the conversation to the few issues that deserve attention first.The couple stops trying to fix everything at once.
Turn intentions into habitsTranslate priorities into practical routines, boundaries, or recurring decisions.The plan feels livable, not idealised.
Define next actions clearlyAgree who will do what, what happens next, and what success looks like in the next period.Both partners leave with clarity rather than assumption.
