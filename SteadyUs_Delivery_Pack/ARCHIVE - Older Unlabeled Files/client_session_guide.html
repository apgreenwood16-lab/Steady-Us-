Couples Budget Reset
Session Guide

This guide gives you a calm, simple overview of what the package is for, what to expect, and how the two sessions work together.

Important scope reminder

This package offers budgeting support and financial organisation only. It does not provide regulated financial, tax, pension, mortgage, or legal advice.

What this package helps with

The Couples Budget Reset is designed to help you understand your money situation together and leave with a clearer shared plan. It is meant to make money conversations feel calmer, clearer, and easier to act on.

What you can expect What this is not
A calmer conversation about money Regulated financial advice
Support to get organised together Investment or pension recommendations
Clearer priorities and next steps Debt counselling or legal support
A written summary after the sessions Therapy or crisis support

Before Session 1

You do not need perfect spreadsheets, perfect paperwork, or perfect answers before you begin. The aim is to create clarity from where you are now, not from some ideal starting point.

Step What to do
Book your time Confirm a time that works for both of you if possible.
Complete the intake form Share the basic details requested and a few suggested times.
Keep preparation light You do not need a polished budget or a finished system before the first session.
Arrive ready to talk honestly It helps to know what feels unclear, stressful, or repeatedly postponed.

What happens in Session 1

The first session is about understanding the current picture. We slow things down, make your money situation more visible, and identify the areas that feel most confusing, heavy, or stuck.

Session 1 focus What it means
Clarify the current picture Understand what is happening with income, spending, obligations, savings, and current pressure points.
Surface what feels heavy Notice where money feels tense, unclear, or repeatedly avoided.
Identify the main sticking points Work out what keeps creating friction or making decisions harder than they need to be.
Leave with focus Finish with a clearer sense of what matters most to work on next.

Between sessions

Between the two sessions, the aim is to keep momentum light and useful. You may receive a few simple follow-up questions or prompts. This should not feel like heavy homework.

Reminder What this means for you
Keep it simple Brief reflection is enough. There is no need to build a complete system on your own.
Notice what matters Pay attention to what still feels urgent, unclear, or most important.
Stay realistic The goal is practical progress, not a perfect plan overnight.

What happens in Session 2

The second session turns clarity into action. We use what became visible in Session 1 to agree realistic priorities, next steps, and habits that fit real life.

Session 2 focus What it means
Agree the priorities Decide which issues matter most to tackle first.
Build a realistic plan Turn intentions into actions and routines that feel manageable.
Clarify who does what Make responsibilities and next steps visible rather than assumed.
Leave with a shared direction Finish with a plan you can both understand and follow.

After the sessions

After the package, you receive a written summary designed to reinforce what you agreed rather than overwhelm you with a long report.

What you receive Purpose
Priority recap A reminder of the main issues you agreed matter most right now.
Next-step summary A clear record of the actions or decisions you made together.
Focus points A short reminder of what to keep returning to after the sessions.
Optional follow-up details Information about extra check-ins if you want continued support.
You do not need to have everything figured out before the first session. You only need to be willing to look at the situation honestly and work through it together.
