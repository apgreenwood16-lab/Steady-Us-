# SteadyUs Client Session Checklist

**Version date:** 2026-05-17  
**Audience:** Client delivery source

This checklist is a simple client-facing guide for the **Couples Budget Reset**. It is designed to make the process feel clear and manageable from the first booking message through to the final written summary. The tone is intentionally calm and practical so couples know they do not need to arrive with perfect finances, perfect paperwork, or perfect answers.

## What this package is for

The Couples Budget Reset is designed to help you understand your money situation together and leave with a clearer shared plan. It focuses on budgeting support and financial organisation. It does not provide regulated financial, investment, tax, pension, or legal advice.

| What you can expect | What this is not |
| --- | --- |
| A calmer conversation about money | Regulated financial advice |
| Support to get organised together | Investment or pension recommendations |
| Clearer priorities and next steps | Debt counselling or legal support |
| A written summary after the sessions | Therapy or crisis support |

## Before you begin

You do not need to prepare everything in advance. The goal is not to impress anyone or prove that you have your finances under control already. The goal is to create clarity from where you are now.

| Before Session 1 | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Book your time** | Confirm a time that works for both of you if possible. |
| **Complete the intake form** | Share the basic details requested and a few suggested times. |
| **Keep preparation light** | You do not need perfect spreadsheets or a polished budget. |
| **Arrive ready to talk honestly** | It helps to come with a rough sense of what feels unclear, stressful, or repeatedly postponed. |

## What happens in Session 1

The first session is about understanding the current picture. We slow things down, make your money situation more visible, and identify the areas that feel most confusing, heavy, or stuck. The purpose of this session is not to solve everything in one go. It is to help both of you see the same picture together.

| Session 1 focus | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Clarify the current money picture** | Understand what is happening with income, spending, obligations, savings, and current pressure points. |
| **Surface what feels heavy** | Notice where money feels tense, unclear, or repeatedly avoided. |
| **Identify the main sticking points** | Work out what keeps creating friction or making decisions harder than they need to be. |
| **Leave with focus** | Finish with a clearer sense of what matters most to work on next. |

## Between sessions

Between the two sessions, the aim is to keep the momentum light and useful. You may be asked a few simple follow-up questions or prompts. This should not feel like heavy homework. It is simply a way to help Session 2 be more focused and practical.

| Between-session reminder | What this means for you |
| --- | --- |
| **Keep it simple** | Brief reflection is enough. No need to build a complete system on your own. |
| **Notice what matters** | Pay attention to what still feels urgent, unclear, or most important. |
| **Stay realistic** | The goal is practical progress, not a perfect plan overnight. |

## What happens in Session 2

The second session turns clarity into action. We take what became visible in Session 1 and use it to agree realistic priorities, next steps, and habits that fit real life. This is where the conversation becomes more practical and structured.

| Session 2 focus | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Agree the priorities** | Decide which issues matter most to tackle first. |
| **Build a realistic plan** | Turn intentions into actions and routines that feel manageable. |
| **Clarify who does what** | Make responsibilities and next steps visible, rather than assumed. |
| **Leave with a shared direction** | Finish with a plan you can both understand and follow. |

## After the sessions

After the package, you receive a written summary to help keep things practical. This is designed to reinforce what you agreed rather than overwhelm you with a long report.

| What you receive | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Priority recap** | A reminder of the main issues you agreed matter most right now. |
| **Next-step summary** | A clear record of the actions or decisions you made together. |
| **Focus points** | A short reminder of what to keep returning to after the sessions. |
| **Optional follow-up details** | Information about extra check-ins if you want continued support. |

## A helpful reminder before you start

You do not need to have everything figured out before the first session. You only need to be willing to look at the situation honestly and work through it together. The package is designed to make money conversations feel clearer, calmer, and easier to act on.
