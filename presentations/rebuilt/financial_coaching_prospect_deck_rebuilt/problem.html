Common pattern

Why sensible couples still stay stuck

Most couples do not avoid money because they do not care. They avoid it because the conversation feels loaded, repetitive, or harder to start than either partner wants to admit.
The result is often a low-grade feeling of drift: you both mean to sort it out, but everyday life keeps getting there first.
01

The conversation keeps getting postponed

Money only comes up when there is stress, a deadline, or a small disagreement that suddenly carries more weight than it should.

02

The same tensions come round again

Spending, savings, debt, or house goals stay emotionally noisy because there is no shared structure holding the discussion together.

03

One or both partners feel mentally overloaded

Even when both people care, the planning and remembering can quietly fall onto one person, which creates more friction over time.
