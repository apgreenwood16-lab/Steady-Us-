Notes and follow-up

Document clearly and close with usable next steps

Good documentation should support clarity, continuity, and calm follow-through. Keep it focused and practical.

Use the notes template to capture what actually matters

What to capture

Record the couple’s tone, main pressure points, recurring friction, and the few issues that matter next.

How to write it

Summarise the pattern plainly; focus on clarity, uneven load, avoidance, decisions, and ownership.

What to avoid

Avoid data dumps, moral verdicts, or pseudo-advice. Keep notes to the essentials.

Run a quick delivery quality check after each session

Tone check

Was the conversation calm, practical, and balanced for both partners?

Scope check

Did the session stay within budgeting and organisation, not specialist advice?

Outcome check

Did they leave clearer with a specific next step to act on?

Written follow-up should stay concise and practical

Reinforce the main priorities and agreed next steps in plain language. Keep it short and actionable; add a brief scope reminder if needed.
