Session 1 flow

Session 1 makes the picture visible

The first session should slow the conversation down, bring the real money picture into view, and help both partners name the patterns that keep creating drag. Stay curious, steady, and selective rather than trying to fix everything at once.
Stage 01

Open the conversation

Set a calm tone, reinforce that the goal is clarity rather than blame, and make it easier for both partners to contribute without defensiveness.

Stage 02

Map the current picture

Bring income, spending, savings, obligations, and immediate pressures into one shared view so the couple can describe reality in simple terms.

Stage 03

Surface pressure points

Notice where money feels heavy, avoided, emotionally loaded, or repeatedly postponed so hidden stress becomes discussable.

Stage 04

Identify friction patterns

Highlight the recurring loops such as confusion, overload, uneven responsibility, or repeated disagreements without turning the conversation into fault-finding.

Stage 05

Close with focus

Summarise the few issues that matter most and frame what Session 2 needs to solve next so the couple leaves with direction, not overwhelm.

Desired outcome

By the end of Session 1, the couple should have a shared starting point: a clearer sense of what is really going on, what is causing friction, and which topics deserve focused attention next.

Internal prompts that keep the session grounded

Clarify

“If we slowed everything down for a moment, what feels most financially unclear right now?”

Reduce blame

“What feels hard about the system or situation, rather than about either of you as a person?”

Prepare Session 2

“If next session could make one part of this feel more manageable, what would matter most?”
