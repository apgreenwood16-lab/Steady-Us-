Before Session 1

Reduce friction before the first call

The pre-session job is not to collect perfect information. It is to help the couple arrive feeling safe enough to be honest and clear enough to know what the first conversation is for.

A good start lowers emotional load, keeps preparation light, and frames the session as shared work rather than one partner being assessed.
01

Lower emotional pressure

Use simple reassurance early. Make it explicit that the first session is about clarity, not judgement, and that they do not need perfect behaviour or perfect records to begin.

02

Set expectations clearly

Frame Session 1 as a visibility session. The aim is to understand the current picture and surface the main pressure points, not to solve every financial issue immediately.

03

Reduce preparation burden

Ask only for light preparation or a short intake response. Avoid turning the lead-in into a demanding admin exercise that makes the couple feel behind before they start.

04

Encourage joint attendance

Position the session as something both partners benefit from hearing together. This helps create shared context from the outset and reduces one-sided retelling later.
